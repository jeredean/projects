Key Concepts

Tokens: Small chunks of text (characters, subwords, or words) the model reads and writes. Billing and limits are measured in tokens, not characters.

Parameters: The learned weights inside the model. More parameters can increase capacity, but quality also depends on data, training, and architecture.

Context Window: The maximum number of tokens the model can attend to in one request (prompt + output). Larger windows help with long documents and conversations.

Temperature / Top-p: Controls randomness. Lower values produce more deterministic answers; higher values increase creativity and diversity.

Function / Tool Calling: The model returns structured arguments to call your functions or tools (search, databases, code execution), enabling agentic workflows.

Multimodal: Ability to handle multiple input/output types beyond text (e.g. images, audio). Some families include dedicated vision or speech variants.

Instruction Tuning vs Fine-tuning: Instruction-tuned models follow natural language directions. Fine-tuning adapts a model further on your domain-specific data.

Embeddings: Numeric vector representations of text for search, clustering, RAG, and semantic similarity.

Inferencing: Running a trained model to produce outputs from prompts; speed and cost depend on hardware, quantization, batching, and whether responses stream tokens as they are generated.

Throughput & Latency: Throughput is tokens processed per second; latency is time-to-first-token. Both matter for user experience and cost.

Safety & Alignment: Techniques (RLHF, policy training, filters) that guide models to follow safe, helpful behavior and avoid harmful outputs.

The Future of LLMs

As LLMs continue to evolve, we're moving toward more specialized, efficient, and capable models. The future holds multimodal AI that can seamlessly process text, images, audio, and video, creating more natural and intuitive interactions.

We're also seeing developments in AI agents that can perform complex tasks autonomously, better reasoning capabilities that approach human-level performance, and more accessible AI that democratizes advanced technology for everyone.

The impact on society will be profound, from solving climate change challenges to advancing scientific research, LLMs are becoming essential tools for addressing humanity's greatest challenges while enhancing our daily experiences.
